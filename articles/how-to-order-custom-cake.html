How to Order a Custom Cake in Hyderabad: Step by Step

Ordering a custom cake should not be complicated. But for first-timers, the process can feel intimidating. Here is the complete step-by-step guide to ordering a custom cake in Hyderabad — what to ask, what to provide, and what to expect.

Step 1: Collect Reference Images

Before you contact any bakery, save 3-5 reference images of cakes you like. Use Pinterest, Instagram, or Google Images. The references do not need to be perfect — they help the baker understand your taste, colour preferences, and the overall vibe you are going for.

Tip: Save images that show the cake from multiple angles, especially the top and side. These details matter.

Step 2: Pick a Date

The date of the event determines everything else — design complexity, flavour, delivery time. Be clear about:

  • The event date
  • The time you want the cake delivered
  • The location of delivery (this affects logistics)

Step 3: Estimate the Headcount

How many people will eat the cake? This determines the size:

  • 10 people: 1 kg
  • 15-20 people: 1.5-2 kg
  • 30 people: 3 kg
  • 50 people: 5 kg
  • 100+ people: 10 kg or multi-tier

A 1 kg cake yields about 10-12 generous slices. For dessert portions (smaller), the count is higher.

Custom cake decoration takes hours of detailed work — plan your timeline accordingly.

Step 4: Choose the Flavour

You will need to specify the cake flavour separately from the design. Flavour options at most good bakeries include:

  • Chocolate truffle, Black Forest, vanilla, butterscotch, strawberry, pineapple, mango, fresh fruit
  • Premium options: tiramisu, Lotus Biscoff, hazelnut praline, salted caramel
  • Indian fusion: Rasmalai, Gulab Jamun, motichoor laddoo, kesar pista

Step 5: Communicate the Design Brief

This is where most miscommunications happen. Be specific:

  • Colour palette: "Soft pastels with a touch of gold" is better than "pink"
  • Theme: "Mickey Mouse" leaves much undefined. "Mickey Mouse in vintage 1930s style with red and white" is much clearer
  • Text: Provide the exact text and how you want it placed. Spell names carefully — bakers cannot guess spelling
  • Toppers and decorations: Mention specifically — number toppers, edible photos, fondant figurines, etc.
  • What NOT to include: If you do not want sprinkles or fondant figurines, say so

Step 6: Ask About Pricing Upfront

Get a clear quote in writing or via WhatsApp before confirming. The quote should include:

  • Cake price (based on weight and flavour)
  • Decoration surcharge (for elaborate fondant work)
  • Toppers and figurines (often charged separately)
  • Delivery fee
  • Total including GST

Step 7: Make the Booking

Most bakeries require a deposit (often 50%) to confirm a custom cake order. Pay this and keep the receipt. The order is now confirmed.

Step 8: Stay Available for Clarifications

The baker may have follow-up questions:

  • Spelling of names
  • Exact font style
  • Specific colour shades (Pantone codes can be useful for serious matches)
  • Allergies or dietary restrictions

Respond to these promptly. Delays here cause delays in the cake.

Step 9: The Delivery

Be ready at the agreed delivery time. The cake will arrive in a sturdy box — open it gently and inspect immediately. If anything looks wrong, take photos right away and contact the bakery.

Step 10: After the Event

If the cake was great, leave a review online and tag the bakery on Instagram. Word of mouth is how good bakeries grow.

Common First-Time Mistakes to Avoid

  • Ordering too late. Custom cakes need at least 48 hours. Elaborate ones need 72 hours or more
  • Being vague about design. Vague briefs lead to disappointing results
  • Not confirming spelling. Always send the exact spelling in writing
  • Cheap-quote shopping. Quality custom cakes have a price. If a quote is dramatically lower than others, ask why
  • Skipping the deposit. Unconfirmed orders can fall off the bakery's schedule
