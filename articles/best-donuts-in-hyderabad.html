What Makes a Great Donut

1. Same-Day Fried

Donuts go stale within 12 hours. A great donut shop fries fresh every morning. We start frying at 5 AM at every outlet.

2. The Right Dough

Good donut dough rises slowly (often overnight) to develop flavour and the characteristic light, airy interior. Fast-rise donuts are denser and chewier — not what you want.

3. Quality Oil

Donuts should be fried in clean, high-quality oil at a precise temperature (175-180°C). Old oil makes donuts greasy and dark.

4. Proper Glaze Technique

Glaze should be applied while donuts are slightly warm — too hot and it slides off, too cold and it cracks.

5. Fillings That Are Fresh

For filled donuts, the filling should be made daily. Pre-prepared filling sitting in containers for days loses freshness.

Eggless Donuts at Krispie's

About 60% of our donuts are eggless by default. The eggless donut technique requires careful balance of milk, oil, and leavening agents. Done properly, eggless donuts are as light and fluffy as egg-based ones. No extra charge for eggless versions.
