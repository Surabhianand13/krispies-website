Gender Reveal Cakes — The Hottest New Way to Share Big News

Gender reveal cakes are exactly what they sound like. The colour inside the cake — pink for girl, blue for boy — reveals the baby's gender when the cake is cut. The dramatic moment has made these cakes one of our fastest-growing categories.

How It Works

The bakery is given the gender information confidentially (usually after the 20-week anatomy scan, when the gender can be reliably determined). The cake is baked with pink or blue sponge inside while the exterior is decorated in neutral colours like white, cream, or yellow.

At the gender reveal party, the parents-to-be cut the cake together. The colour of the sponge inside reveals the baby's gender to everyone — including, sometimes, to the parents themselves if they have chosen to be surprised.

The "Don't Tell Me, Just Bake It" Order

Many couples want the cake to be a surprise even for themselves. Here is how it works at Krispie's:

  1. Your obstetrician sends us the gender information directly via email or sealed envelope
  2. We bake the cake with the appropriate colour inside
  3. You receive the cake without knowing the result
  4. You cut it at the party and find out alongside your guests

This is the most popular way of ordering gender reveal cakes in 2026.

Neutral on the outside, surprise colour inside — the gender reveal cake's signature design.

Common Gender Reveal Cake Designs

The Question Mark Cake

A round white or yellow cake with a giant fondant "?" on top. When cut, the colour inside answers the question.

The "Boy or Girl?" Cake

Two-tone fondant — half pink, half blue on the outside, with the slogan "Boy or Girl?" in the middle. The inside reveals the answer.

The Balloon Pop Cake

A cake topped with a fondant balloon. When cut, the cake colour shows the answer (rather than popping the balloon).

The "He or She?" Banner Cake

A cake decorated with a fondant banner reading "He or She?" with the colour inside answering.

Recommended Flavours

Since the inside colour is doing the work, the cake flavour should be one that takes colour well:

  • Vanilla sponge — Takes pink or blue colouring beautifully
  • Strawberry sponge — Naturally pink, perfect if the baby is a girl (or surprise!)
  • Coconut sponge — Soft white base that holds colour well
  • Lemon sponge — Subtle tang, light texture

Avoid chocolate sponge — the dark colour overwhelms any pink or blue food colouring.

Party Logistics

For maximum drama, plan the cake-cutting carefully:

  • Gather everyone around before cutting
  • Have someone film with their phone
  • Cut the cake slowly enough that the colour is revealed clearly
  • Take a wide shot of the reactions, not just the cake

Cultural Sensitivity in India

In India, knowing a baby's gender before birth is a sensitive subject due to historical concerns about gender-based selection. Pre-natal gender determination is legally restricted by the PCPNDT Act.

However, many parents choose to learn the gender after the legal viability window and share it discreetly with family for planning purposes. The gender reveal cake is a way to share that information joyfully with extended family — it does not violate any laws when the information is shared after birth or in a private family setting.

The "Twin Reveal" Cake

For parents expecting twins, the cake can be made with two coloured halves — boy/girl, boy/boy, or girl/girl. The cut reveals both at once. Some of our most emotional cake deliveries have been twin gender reveals.
