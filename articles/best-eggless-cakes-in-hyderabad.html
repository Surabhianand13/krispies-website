How Eggless Cake Baking Works (The Science)

What Eggs Do in a Cake

  1. Structure — egg proteins coagulate to give the cake firmness
  2. Rise — beaten egg whites trap air for lightness
  3. Moisture and Richness — yolks add fat and emulsion

How We Replace Each Function

  • For Structure: Cake flour + condensed milk + tiny cornflour
  • For Rise: Baking soda + baking powder + buttermilk/yoghurt/vinegar (acidic activation)
  • For Moisture: Oil + full-fat milk + yoghurt + cream cheese

Done right, this produces cakes that are equally fluffy, moist, and rich as their egg-based counterparts. Read the deep dive on eggless baking.
